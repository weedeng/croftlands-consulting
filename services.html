Graeme has operated as interim CFO across a range of PE-backed and founder-led SMEs with direct experience of investor reporting, liquidity management, acquisition integration, and finance team stabilisation. He joins as a full member of the board or senior management team, accountable for all financial outcomes during the engagement.

  • Financial control, governance, and internal checks

  • Transaction readiness & due diligence

Graeme combines board-level commercial strategy with modern, AI-enhanced financial tools — handling everything from 3-statement forecasting and investor management to automated reporting workflows.

  • Investor relations, cap table & lender management
  • Board pack creation & board attendance
  • Automated BI dashboards & KPI tracking
  • Unit economics, pricing & margin optimisation

Service 03

Finance Function Build-Out

For growth-stage businesses whose finance function was built for a much smaller operation and has not scaled with the business — or for early-stage companies that need to implement a proper finance infrastructure for the first time.

This typically involves selecting and implementing an ERP (or upgrading the existing system), designing the chart of accounts and reporting hierarchy, building the management reporting pack, establishing internal controls, and putting in place the team and processes to support continued growth. The goal is a finance function that is credible to investors, reliable to management, and capable of operating without constant firefighting.

What this typically includes

  • ERP selection and implementation
  • Chart of accounts design
  • Month-end close process design (T+5 to T+10)
  • Board reporting pack design
  • KPI and unit economics dashboard
  • Internal controls and approval matrix
  • Finance team structure and hiring plan
  • Accounting policies documentation
Service 04

AI and Advanced Analytics

Machine learning models and LLM-powered analytical systems that replace static Excel reports with predictive dashboards and intelligent automation. As a graduate of the MIT Professional Education Applied AI & Data Science Program (8 courses, 61 working models), Graeme applies these techniques directly to client mandates — combining CFO-level commercial judgment with rigorous statistical methodology.

For SaaS and recurring revenue businesses, this means XGBoost or Random Forest churn risk models that score customer accounts weekly, paired with K-Means clustering to drive pricing and upsell strategy. For any business, it means board pack commentary automatically generated from live actuals data — providing automated variance narratives each morning, rather than manual assembly the night before a board meeting.

What this typically includes

  • Predictive churn risk scoring (Logistic Regression / XGBoost / Random Forest)
  • LLM-generated board pack commentary & variance analysis (enterprise LLM APIs)
  • Customer segmentation for pricing and upsell (K-Means clustering)
  • Interactive BI dashboards & automated data pipelines (Python / SQL)
  • ARR waterfall, cohort retention, and LTV:CAC analytics
  • Lead conversion probability scoring and pipeline forecasting
  • Product affinity and cross-sell modelling
  • Automated financial data integration & ETL workflows
Service 05

Process Automation

Eliminating the manual, repetitive tasks that consume finance teams and introduce human error — by building lightweight, robust integrations and automated pipelines between the systems your business already uses.

Common examples include automated multi-entity consolidation into a group ERP, deferred revenue recognition engines fed by billing outputs, and automated CRM-to-ledger pipelines that eliminate manual data entry.

Graeme also builds LLM-powered workflows using enterprise LLM APIs — generating management commentary, variance narratives, and executive summaries directly from live actuals data, rather than being written manually at month-end. All tools are custom-built in Python and JavaScript, designed to be lightweight and fully maintainable by the finance team without relying on internal IT support.

What this typically includes

  • CRM-to-ledger pipeline automation
  • LLM-generated variance narratives & board commentary (enterprise LLM APIs)
  • Automated multi-entity ERP consolidation
  • Deferred revenue & subscription recognition engines
  • Automated billing system & payment gateway reconciliation
  • Month-end close workflow automation
  • Custom Python & JavaScript financial data scripts
  • Automated exception reporting & data validation audits

Graeme bridges this gap by wearing two hats simultaneously — combining 25 years of CFO leadership with MIT-trained Applied AI, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning capability.

  • Strategic "Build vs. Buy" Assessment & AI Tech Stack Rationalisation

Service 08

Audit & Statutory Reporting

The year-end audit and the statutory accounts are where a finance function is judged — and where an unprepared one loses weeks and credibility. Croftlands Consulting gets the company audit-ready, runs the year-end process end to end, and prepares statutory financial statements under FRS 102 or IFRS.

The same discipline starts at month-end: a clean, timely close is what makes year-end painless, so the two are delivered together. Graeme has managed group statutory audits, resolved a material prior-year revenue restatement mid-audit, and achieved zero audit adjustments on two acquisitions in a subsequent FTSE 100 group audit — with auditors from the Big 4 through to mid-tier firms.

What this typically includes

  • Audit preparedness review
  • Year-end audit management & auditor liaison
  • Prepared-by-client (PBC) schedules & evidence
  • Statutory financial statements (FRS 102 / IFRS)
  • Month-end close process design (T+5 to T+10)
  • Balance sheet reconciliations & controls
  • Going concern & disclosure support
  • Prior-year restatement resolution

Service 10

Growth Modelling & Cashflow Forecasting

Sophisticated financial modelling that goes beyond the standard Excel budget — using regression, scenario planning, and sensitivity analysis to build forecasts that genuinely reflect the drivers of your business rather than last year’s actuals multiplied by a percentage.

The 12-week rolling cashflow model is built from first principles — a receipts-and-payments model maintained with weekly discipline and daily cash management rigour. For SaaS, ARR and churn forecasting is built on cohort data and regression analysis, enabling boards to model the impact of pricing changes, product launches, and sales team changes with precision. Scenario analysis (base, stress, upside) is built in from day one.

What this typically includes

  • 12-week rolling cashflow model
  • Scenario analysis (base / stress / upside)
  • ARR waterfall and churn forecast
  • Regression-based revenue model
  • GTM financial modelling
  • Price increase impact modelling
  • New location roll-out modelling
  • Long-range planning (3–5 year)
