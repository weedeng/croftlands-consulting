Founder & Principal

Graeme Weeden — Senior Finance Leader, CFO Advisor & Applied Data Scientist

Graeme Weeden is a Chartered Accountant (South Africa) with over 25 years of experience operating at board and executive level across private equity-backed, acquisitive and complex multi-entity businesses in the United Kingdom and South Africa.

His career began at KPMG in Johannesburg, where he qualified as a Chartered Accountant before moving to London and building experience in investment banking — as a product controller and treasury risk manager at Lehman Brothers, UBS Warburg, CSFB and EBRD — before returning to South Africa to take on C-suite roles at ABN AMRO and Coronation Capital.

Since 2010, Graeme has operated primarily as a finance consultant and interim CFO to SMEs and growth businesses, with recent senior engagements at Dext Software (global PE-backed SaaS), Informa plc (FTSE 100) and Raccoon Media Group (PE-backed international events).

Graeme is a graduate of the MIT Professional Education Applied AI & Data Science Program (2026 cohort, 8 courses, 61 working models) — applying machine learning techniques including XGBoost churn prediction, K-Means and hierarchical customer segmentation, BERT-based NLP, and LLM pipeline automation directly to client mandates.

MIT Programme — Applied to Finance

The MIT Applied AI & Data Science Program is an 8-course, hands-on curriculum taught by MIT faculty including Prof. Caroline Uhler (unsupervised learning, network analysis), Prof. Munther Dahleh (ensemble methods) and Prof. Devavrat Shah (recommendation systems). Below is how each module maps to finance applications deployed in client work.

Pre-Work
Generative AI Foundations
LLM pipeline (Llama-2) for banking customer intent classification — foundation for automated board commentary and document summarisation.
Course 1
Statistical Foundations & Python
Hypothesis testing, probability, statistical inference applied to financial data — underpins all analytical work.
Course 2
Unsupervised Learning & Network Analysis
PCA, t-SNE, K-Means, DBSCAN, GMM and hierarchical clustering. Applied to customer segmentation, pricing strategy and account retention prioritisation. Network analysis (NetworkX / PageRank) for relationship mapping.
Course 3
Supervised Learning
KNN, logistic regression, SVM, cross-validation. Employee attrition prediction (maps directly to talent risk in PE-backed businesses), sales forecasting via regression.
Course 4
Ensemble Methods & Classification
Random Forest, XGBoost, AdaBoost, Gradient Boosting — applied to classification problems including churn prediction, loan default and employee attrition. The ensemble framework underpinning the SaaS churn risk scoring model.
Course 5
Deep Learning
ANNs, CNNs, Transformers in PyTorch. BERT fine-tuning for text classification. Transfer learning (VGG16, EfficientNet). Applied to document classification and financial text analytics.
Course 6
Recommendation Systems
Collaborative filtering (KNN, SVD), matrix factorisation. Applied to product affinity and cross-sell modelling for SaaS.
Capstone
End-to-End Projects
Loan default prediction (own work, Random Forest). Marketing campaign customer segmentation (K-Means / DBSCAN / GMM). Real-Time Retail Feedback Intelligence — LLM pipeline with Zero-Shot, Few-Shot and Chain-of-Thought prompting, LLM-as-judge evaluation.
